concept

The name of the way light is distorted by heat became the name of the way sound is.

Swelter is a live-input insert effect. Whatever you feed its line-in — an instrument, a mix, a voice — passes through a fixed chain of six optical lenses that waver, detune, saturate, and age it, then blends back against the dry signal. The engine is the sole path the input takes to the main output, so the wet/dry balance lives entirely under your thumb on E1.

The vocabulary is borrowed straight from the physics of a hot horizon. Air over a baking road is less dense, so it bends light unevenly and the world behind it shimmers, doubles, and dissolves. Swelter does the same to sound — each optical term maps onto a concrete piece of DSP.

six lenses

refraction

light bending as it crosses moving layers of air

dspa delay line modulated by a slow wow LFO summed with a fast flutter LFO; L/R run at offset phase
sonicpitch wander — the tape transport breathing, slow drift under fast quiver

heat haze

the shimmering waver hanging over a hot surface

dspa sine↔noise ripple (blend = turbulence) applies an amplitude dip plus a micro pitch ripple at a settable rate
sonica restless, heat-mirage flutter on top of the signal; also drives the screen's shimmer

mirage

an image of something that isn't actually there

dspa parallel pitch-shifted voice, detuned a few cents, summed in and level-normalized
sonica ghost double a hair off-pitch — thickening, chorus-adjacent, slightly unreal

saturation

the literal distortion — the heat in the signal

dspgain-compensated tanh soft-clip, pre-gain set by drive
sonictape warmth into grit — rounded edges first, harmonic dirt as you push

tape age

worn playback, dulled and hissing

dspa low-pass whose cutoff drops with age, plus a summed pink-noise hiss bed
sonichighs roll off and a noise floor creeps in — decades of wear on a dial

dropout

creased, shedding tape losing contact with the head

dsprandom triggers fire short level sags; one knob scales how often and how deep
sonicintermittent stumbles in the level, like a worn cassette skipping its grip

signal chain

Order follows tape signal flow — record, transport, playback — with the two creative optical layers woven into the middle.

in · trim → saturation → refraction → + mirage → heat-haze → dropout → tape-age → dry/wet → out

One stereo SuperCollider SynthDef. Every value is smoothed (Lag.kr) so fast knob moves never click.

controls

Page-cycled, in the muscle memory of Yarn. E1 is always dry/wet. K2 steps the page; the readout surfaces, then fades. K3 held is momentary bypass for A/B. K1 held lets E2/E3 reach the underlying rates without leaving the play screen.

screen

128×64, layered. A continuously animating heat-haze field sits behind everything, its scanlines bending harder as input level and modulation rise. A param readout surfaces on any encoder or key activity and fades back into the haze after about a second and a half.

mock — readout visible. Idle, only the haze field remains, wavering.
